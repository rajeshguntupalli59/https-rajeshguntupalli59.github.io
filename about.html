About

I specialize in Microsoft SQL Server — diagnosing performance problems, designing indexing strategies, and helping engineering teams understand what their databases are actually doing under load.

Every tool here started as something I needed myself. Not a product pitch — a fix for a problem that kept recurring, built because the existing options were too expensive, too cloud-dependent, or simply didn't exist.

The books follow the same principle: direct and technical, written from real production SQL Server experience — not theory dressed up as advice.

Everything is self-hosted by design. Your schema, your queries, and your credentials stay on your infrastructure — that's not a feature, it's the starting requirement for everything I build.

Lessons Learned the Hard Way

01

An index on the wrong column is worse than no index — it creates false confidence and burns I/O.

02

An execution plan is an estimate, not a guarantee. Actual runtime behavior diverges from estimates at scale.

03

Schema documentation written manually is always out of date. Generate it from the live database.

04

The most expensive query you have right now is probably not where you expect it to be.

05

Monitoring that pages you at 3am is better than a dashboard nobody checks.
